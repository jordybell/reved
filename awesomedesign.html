15 october 2015

Jordan Bell

What is UX design?

All things being fair, I think it’s important to start by saying there’s conflicting perspectives over the nuance of the meaning. User Experience Design is a concept that encapsulates many perspectives & several disciplines
—Such as interaction design, information architecture, visual design, usability, and human-computer interaction.
Downt to brass tacks - UX design is the process of designing (digital or tangible) products that are useful, easy to use, and delightful to interact with. Interact, being the fulcrum word, around which all ancillary concepts orbit.

Every Successful UX is arrived at by following an ever evolving process based around 2 principle (Specifically...at Reverb)

Kanban
and
Kaizen

Kanban is a scheduling system for lean and just-in-time (JIT) production. Kanban is a system to control the logistical chain from a production point of view, and is an process control system. In other words, an optimized workflow.

Kaizen , Which has a literal translation of Change & Good, is the practice of continuous improvement. Today Kaizen is recognized worldwide as an important pillar of an organization's long-term competitive strategy.
